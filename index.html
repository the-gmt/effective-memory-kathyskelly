My Story

From Bedside to Boardroom

I didn't begin my career in a corner office. I began it at the bedside of a child fighting cancer — and that starting point has shaped every decision I've made as a leader since.

After completing my nursing diploma at Algonquin College, I took my first position at the Children's Hospital of Eastern Ontario in Ottawa, working in paediatric oncology. It was there — helping children and families navigate some of the most difficult experiences of their lives — that I discovered what mattered most to me in healthcare: not just delivering care, but building the systems, teams, and culture that make extraordinary care possible.

1999
Joined The Hospital for Sick Children in Toronto, where I would spend a decade in paediatric oncology, eventually leading clinical orientation programs and serving as charge nurse across the Hematology/Oncology/BMT unit.

A decade at SickKids gave me the clinical foundation and the leadership instincts that would define the rest of my career. I saw firsthand how the right orientation program could set a new nurse up for success — or how a gap in teamwork could put a patient at risk. That's where I first started thinking about healthcare not just as clinical practice, but as systems design.

2008
Moved to Hamilton Health Sciences, where progressive roles in quality improvement, clinical education, and patient flow consulting at McMaster Children's Hospital laid the groundwork for the leap into management.

At McMaster, I helped develop high-fidelity simulation training for trauma and resuscitation, built emergency disaster management protocols, and co-created the Pediatric Emergency Reassessment Clinic — a project that reduced unnecessary return visits and improved the patient experience. Those years taught me that real innovation in healthcare comes from listening to frontline teams and giving them the tools and authority to solve problems.

2012
Took on the Manager role for Joseph Brant Hospital's Emergency Department — a $6M operation with a 200+ person workforce and a major facility redevelopment underway.

Joseph Brant was where leadership became operational: managing complex labour relations across three collective agreements, leading a full ED strategic plan for a new patient tower, and turning pay-for-performance metrics into tangible improvements in patient throughput and care quality. It was the hardest three years of my career, and the most formative.

2015
Joined Trillium Health Partners — Ontario's largest community hospital — to manage and grow the Women's and Children's portfolio, a role that would expand into the Director position she holds today.

In 2015, I joined Trillium Health Partners, where I've now spent over a decade building and leading the Women's and Children's portfolio. From clinical manager to my current role as Director, I've had the privilege of guiding our paediatric and maternal health programs through growth, a global pandemic, and ongoing transformation — always anchored by the same principle that drew me to nursing in the first place: that every patient is someone's child, someone's parent, someone's whole world.

Signature Impact

Leadership in Action

01
Building the Pediatric Emergency Reassessment Clinic
McMaster Children's Hospital, Hamilton Health Sciences

The Challenge

Paediatric emergency patients frequently returned to the ED for reassessment, straining capacity and creating a fragmented experience for families already under stress.

The Approach

Co-designed and implemented the PERC — a dedicated reassessment pathway that redirected follow-up patients to a purpose-built clinic, developed in collaboration with ED leadership and frontline staff.

The Impact

Reduced unnecessary ED return visits, improved patient flow and family satisfaction, and demonstrated a replicable model for paediatric emergency programs.

02
Leading ED Redevelopment at Joseph Brant Hospital
Joseph Brant Hospital, Burlington

The Challenge

A major hospital expansion — the new Michael Lee-Chin and Family Patient Tower — required the Emergency Department to develop a comprehensive strategic and operational plan for a redesigned care environment.

The Approach

Led the full ED strategic plan for redevelopment, balancing daily operational demands of a 200+ person workforce with the design, planning, and stakeholder coordination required for the new facility.

The Impact

Delivered a modernized ED with improvements to input, throughput, and output metrics, and measurable gains in pay-for-performance benchmarks that reflected better patient care and operational efficiency.

03
Pandemic PPE Standardization Across Trillium Health Partners
Trillium Health Partners, Mississauga

The Challenge

When COVID-19 arrived in early 2020, Trillium faced urgent, evolving PPE requirements across a large multi-site organization with thousands of frontline staff.

The Approach

Took on the role of Pandemic PPE Lead, developing standardized protocols for selection, use, and education. Built training programs that could scale quickly while maintaining clinical safety standards.

The Impact

Established a consistent, organization-wide PPE standard during the most critical phase of the pandemic, ensuring staff safety and regulatory compliance amid rapidly changing guidance.

04
Growing Community Partnerships for Paediatric Care
Trillium Health Partners, Mississauga

The Challenge

Children's health services extend far beyond hospital walls. The paediatric program needed community partnerships to deliver holistic care to a diverse, growing population.

The Approach

Built and sustained partnerships with POGO, Ronald McDonald House, Erin Oak Kids, the City of Mississauga, and other stakeholders to create alternative service delivery models and community programming.

The Impact

Expanded the reach of paediatric services through collaborative models, strengthened community engagement through public events, and created sustainable support structures for children and families.
